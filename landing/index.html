I How it works · four movements

A briefing on Monday becomes a chief of staff by Friday.

The wedge is the morning brief. From there, Aria takes on triage, drafting, and meeting capture — under your oversight, at the pace you allow.

i.

Install & connect

A signed app for Mac and Windows. Connect Gmail, Outlook, your calendar, and Todoist. Read scope only at first.

ii.

Read & index

Aria reads the last 90 days locally. Everything is stored in one encrypted SQLite database on your machine. Nothing is uploaded.

iii.

Brief, every morning

By 7am, Aria has written one briefing — agenda, urgent mail, action items, the one thing that could blow up — and is ready when you sit down.

iv.

Earn the leash

Approve a few drafts. Reject the ones that miss your voice. Aria learns. After thirty days you choose how much rope she gets.

II Four deep capabilities

Quiet on the surface. Doing a great deal underneath.

Four products in one application — sharing context, learning together, none of them louder than they need to be.

The Briefing

One page, every morning, before you've finished coffee.

Pulled from calendar, inbox, action items, and the news you follow. Written in the voice of a thoughtful editor. Reads in under two minutes. Surfaces the one thing that could blow up the day at the top.

  • Generated locally; routed to a frontier model with PII redacted
  • Delivered at the time you choose; suspends on sleep, resumes on wake
  • Improves with feedback — "less of this", "more of that"

III The deal we make with you

A premium product makes promises it can keep.

Aria does not earn its place by being clever. It earns its place by being trustworthy. Three commitments that hold for every customer, every feature, every release.

i.

Your data, on your machine.

One encrypted SQLite database. SQLCipher whole-DB AES-256. The key is in your OS keychain. We do not run a server that holds your mail.

ii.

Hybrid routing, by default.

PII and sensitive content route to a small model running locally. Only redacted, scoped context ever reaches a frontier model. Every decision is logged for you to inspect.

iii.

Every send asks you first.

No autonomous outbound communication. No calendar writes without you. The sensitivity classifier holds risky drafts even when you've opted into auto-approve.

What we don't do No telemetry · no analytics · no third-party SDKs · no targeted advertising · no model training on your content · no shadow profile.
